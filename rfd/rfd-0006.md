RFD 0006: Minimalist Metrics Architecture
=========================================

**Author:** Gemini / Chris Seufert

**Ideal Capacity Limitation:** ~200MB RAM / 30-day Retention

1\. Introduction
----------------

We require a metrics collection and storage system capable of operating within a **200MB RAM ceiling** while maintaining a 30-day historical window. This system must support automated event triggering (alerting) and handle approximately 1,020 unique time series.

2\. Motivation: Why VictoriaMetrics?
------------------------------------

While Prometheus is the industry standard, its memory-mapped file architecture and "Head" block management can be volatile in low-RAM environments.

*   **Memory Efficiency:** VictoriaMetrics (VM) does not use a complex write-ahead log (WAL) in the same way Prometheus does; it is designed to compress data immediately, reducing the "active" memory footprint.

*   **Vertical Scalability:** VM is a single binary that handles scraping, storage, and querying, reducing inter-process overhead.

*   **Aggressive Compression:** VM typically achieves **0.4 to 0.6 bytes per sample**, whereas Prometheus often averages 1-2 bytes.


3\. Workload Analysis & Calculations
------------------------------------

### 3.1 Data Points per Interval

*   **System Metrics:** 20 metrics × 1 host = 20 series.

*   **Workload Metrics:** 10 metrics × 100 workloads = 1,000 series.

*   **Total Active Series:** **1,020**


### 3.2 Ingestion Rate Estmates

Assuming a standard **5-second scrape interval** for 20 system metrics, and 10 container metrics x 100 containers:

*   1,020 series×12 samples/min  = 12,240 samples/min

*   12,240×60×24 = 17,625,600 samples/day


### 3.3 Disk Space Projection (30 Days)

Using a conservative estimate of **0.7 bytes per sample** (accounting for index overhead in a small environment):

17,625,600 samples/day × 30 days × 0.7 bytes≈ 353 MB

> **Result:** Even with index overhead and metadata, the total disk footprint for a month of data is **less than 400MB**.

4\. Resource Constraints & Tuning
---------------------------------

To ensure the process stays under the **200MB RAM** limit, the following runtime constraints must be applied:

*   **`GOGC=40`**: Force more frequent garbage collection.

*   **`-memory.allowedPercent=60`**: Limit the internal caches (the most critical flag for VM in small containers).

*   **`-retentionPeriod=1m`**: Match the 30-day requirement.


5\. Event Triggering (The `vmalert` Component)
----------------------------------------------

Events will be handled by the companion binary `vmalert`.

*   **Architecture:** `vmalert` periodically queries VictoriaMetrics. If a condition (e.g., `workload_errors > 0`) is met, it sends a payload to a Webhook or Alertmanager.

*   **Impact:** Running `vmalert` alongside VM will add roughly **30-40MB** of RAM usage, bringing the total expected footprint to **~140MB - 160MB**, safely under the 200MB goal.


6\. Alternatives Considered
---------------------------

*   **Prometheus:** Rejected due to higher idle memory usage and potential OOM (Out of Memory) spikes during block compaction.

*   **InfluxDB 3.0:** Rejected due to the higher overhead of the Arrow/DataFusion engine in sub-GB memory environments.
